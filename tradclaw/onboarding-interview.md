# Onboarding Interview

Use this to personalize Tradclaw. It lives under `tradclaw/` as part of the **setup layer**; the files you will edit afterward are under `workspace/`.

## How to run it

Ask in small batches, not all at once.
Start broad, then narrow.
Skip questions that are clearly irrelevant.

## Batch 1: Household shape
- Who’s in the household?
- Any kids, pets, or elders I should support around?
- What are the 3 biggest household logistics pain points right now?
- What would make this assistant feel immediately useful?

## Batch 2: Calendars and timing
- Which calendars matter most: work, family, school, activities?
- What times of day are most chaotic?
- Do you want a morning brief, afternoon pickup check, evening wrap-up, or some mix?
- What are your quiet hours?

## Batch 3: School and kids
- Do you want school email/message triage?
- Do you want homework photos logged and summarized?
- Should I track recurring school deadlines, spirit days, forms, and supply asks?
- Do you want fun facts or learning snippets in morning briefs?

## Batch 4: Food and home
- Do you want weekly meal planning help?
- Do you keep one shopping list or separate grocery/household lists?
- Do you want reminders about pantry gaps and dinner ingredients?
- What home maintenance tasks are easiest to forget?

## Batch 5: Helpers and household admin
- Any recurring household helpers to track or remind you to pay?
- Do you want recurring reminders for cleaner, sitter, gardener, dog walker, etc.?
- Do you want monthly or seasonal home reset reminders?

## Batch 6: Stories and books
- Do you want custom story generation?
- If yes, what ages, tones, and themes are good?
- Any things to avoid in stories, like scary content or certain topics?
- Do you want a household book inventory and book recommendation flow?

## Batch 7: Boundaries
- What kinds of reminders feel helpful?
- What kinds of reminders feel annoying?
- What should I never automate without asking?
- What information should stay especially private?

## After the interview

Follow the **output contract** in [`apply-interview-results.md`](apply-interview-results.md). At minimum, summarize:

- what you learned  
- which modules to enable now vs leave off  
- which files you will change under `workspace/` (including **`MEMORY.md`** when seeding durable patterns)  
- optional cron ideas
