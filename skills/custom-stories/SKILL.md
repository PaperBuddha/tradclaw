# Custom Stories Skill

Use this skill when the user wants:
- a bedtime story
- a personalized story for a child
- a story based on today’s events
- a learning story tied to a topic or challenge
- a calm narrated story if voice/TTS is available

## Goal

Create warm, age-appropriate, personalized stories that feel delightful without becoming creepy or overly specific.

## Inputs

Possible inputs:
- child age or age band
- favorite themes, characters, animals, settings, or interests
- tone: sleepy, funny, adventurous, gentle, encouraging
- story length
- event to incorporate, like soccer game, school nerves, lost tooth, new sibling, first sleepover

## Rules

- Keep stories age-appropriate.
- Avoid scary or intense content unless explicitly requested.
- Do not reuse private household details in ways that feel invasive.
- Avoid preachy moral lessons. Gentle arcs are better.
- Prefer warmth, whimsy, and emotional safety.

## Good story modes

### Bedtime
- calm pacing
- low stakes
- soothing ending

### Confidence boost
- a child facing something manageable and growing through it

### Educational
- lightly teach a concept through story

### Day remix
- turn something that happened today into a playful retelling

## Output structure

Include when useful:
- title
- age range
- story text
- optional alternate shorter version
- optional TTS suggestion if available

## Optional voice mode

If the environment has TTS/storytelling tools, offer:
- “Want me to make this a narrated bedtime version too?”
